In the early 14th century around 1333 Breda is walled. One can see the city in and out through three gates, the End port at the intersection Eindstraat-Karnemelkstraat, the Hospital Gate at the intersection of the Veemarktstraat and Vlasmarkt and the Toll Bridge Gate near the harbor at the end of the Tolbrugstraat. Remnants of the foundations to see the city wall in the Valkenberg Park. Soon, the city expands beyond the gates and walls to be replaced by fortifications. In the Middle Ages Breda small trading town of approximately 30 hectares. In the 14th century there were approximately 2,700 people.
